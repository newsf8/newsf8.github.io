Ireland rugby legend Brian O’Driscoll appears on the Late Late Show tonight, and will chat with guest presenter Miriam O’Callaghan.

The former Leinster centre hung up the boots in 2014 after a stellar career and is regarded as one of the greatest rugby players of all time.

But the Dubliner has remained close to the game since his retirement. Here’s everything you need to know about the Ireland rugby legend:

What does Brian O’Driscoll do now?

The rugby icon has stayed close to the sport in the years since his retirement.

O’Driscoll is an active rugby pundit, and a regular with BT Sport on TV as well as Off The Ball on radio.

He’s also a brand ambassador with HSBC Sport and Land Rover Rugby and is the co-founder of the Ultimate Rugby app and website.

How old is he?

He turned 41 in January.

Who is his wife?

(Image: Conor McCabe Photography)

O’Driscoll is married to actress Amy Huberman.

The pair tied the knot in Mohill, Leitrim back in 2010.

Does he have children?

The couple have two children together.

Their first child, their daughter Sadie, was born on 10 February 2013.

Their second child, the son Billy, was born on 20 November 2014.

Net worth?

There are no official estimates of O’Driscoll’s net worth.

But in 2018, his firm announced profits of €6.5 million.
