Robbie Williams, 46, refuses to colour his hair as he thinks he’s too old

ROBBIE Williams reckons veteran rock stars look daft colouring their hair and should go natural — like him.

The 46-year-old no longer dyes his locks, believing he is now too old.

3 Robbie Williams says he is too old to keep dying his hair after years of covering up his natural greys Credit: Splash News

3 The 46-year-old said that veteran rockers who continue to dye their hair look daft Credit: Rex Features

He said: “There’s a certain age that you reach where you go, ‘I better stop dying this stuff’.

“You don’t want to have that auburny red tip at the end — you see the rock stars of yesterday do this and you go, ‘Oooh’.

“So what I’ve done is shave it all off.”

In recent years, icons Sir Paul McCartney, 77, and Keith Richards, 79, has started to show their natural grey locks after years of dying their hair.

3 The singer admitted that dying his barnet for the past decade left his hair in a bad condition

But the likes of U2 singer Bono, 59, and Bon Jovi guitarist Richie Sambora, 60, continue to hide their greys.

Old Before I Die singer Robbie admits colouring his hair for the past decade had a negative impact on his barnet.

He added: “I have dyed my hair quite a few times and now my head is bereft of all the hair I used to have.”

Robbie was talking in the US while promoting his shows in Las Vegas.
