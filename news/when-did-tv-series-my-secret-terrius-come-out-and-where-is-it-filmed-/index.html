When did TV series My Secret Terrius come out and where is it filmed?

Some viewers have said the show predicted the current pandemic (Picture: Netflix)

Netflix viewers have been left stunned after watching the Korean series My Secret Terrius, which appeared to predict the coronavirus pandemic.

The Korean romcom, whose cast includes Ji-sub, Jung In-sun, Son Ho-jun and Im Se-mi shows an episode where a coronavirus is mutated to be used as a weapon.

In episode 10 of the series, a doctor is seen saying: ‘Someone tweaked it to increase the mortality rate to 90%.’

‘What’s more serious,’ she adds: ‘Is that the coronavirus has an incubation period of two to 14 days.’

If you want to find the scene in question, simply skip to 53 seconds in on episode 10.

Of course, coronaviruses have been around for years and the current strain doesn’t (thankfully) have a mortality rate of 90%, nor has it been purposely mutated.

Viewers have been gripped (Picture: Netflix)

But that’s not stopped some viewers from freaking out, with people taking to Twitter in their masses to convey their disbelief.

So, when was the show actually created?

When was My Secret Terrius filmed?

The television series, which released in 2018, tells the story of a secret service agent who attempts to unravel the mystery behind a neighbour’s death.

The show was filmed in South Korea and Poland, with some scenes being shot in Warsaw.

Korean dramaland lists some of the filming locations as including the following: Warsaw Chopin Airport, Krzywe Kolo, Warsaw, Wonhyo Bridge, Sejong Center for the Performing Arts, KT&G Seoul Office, Namsan Tower, Savoy City DMC, Lazienki Park and others.

The series is available to stream on Netflix UK now.
