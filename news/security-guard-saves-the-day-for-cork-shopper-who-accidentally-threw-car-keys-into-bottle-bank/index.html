Security guard saves the day for Cork shopper who accidentally threw car keys into bottle bank

A Cork woman has told how a knight in shining armour came to her rescue when her day turned into “pure carnage”.

Gemma from Ballyvolane, was casually emptying glass bottles into a bottle bank near Dunnes Stores in Ballyvolane when her day turned into “pure carnage”.

She had been holding a bit of tissue paper in her hand along with her car keys when she accidentally threw them into the bottle bank, Cork Beo reports.

Speaking to Neil Prendeville on RedFM, Gemma explained how Tom - a security guard at Dunnes- saved the day after “thirty minutes of pure panic”.

After calling her mechanic who told her it would cost at least â‚¬250 on the spot to retrieve the lost keys, Gemma explained how worried she became that she would never see her keys again.

“My heart sank. I stood there for a few minutes thinking what am I going to do?

“I was on the verge of crying.

A panicked Gemma rushed into Dunnes to find a number for the bottle bank company.

She was told the nearest driver’s lorry was already full of glass and therefore couldn’t assist her but could come the following day.

However, they tolf her that the bin in question had been emptied the day before and therefore might be empty enough for a few “strong people” to “tilt over” to retrieve the set of keys.

That’s when she approached security guard Tom on duty in Dunnes Stores - who was happy to help.

After a failed attempt at tilting the bottle bin, security guard suggested using a forklift to lift the bin up and empty it out completely.

“What a gent - he went out of his way to help me”, said Gemma.

The Cork woman told Neil Prendeville that there was glass everywhere and that people were coming over asking to help but Tom told them not to as it was too dangerous.

After some time carefully rummaging through the broken glass - they found the keys!

Gemma went on to say that the same security guard helped her eighteen years ago when her handbag was stolen out of her parked car at Dunnes Stores.

“This man really deserves to be thanked. Tom - if you’re listening, thanks a million!".
