James Adarryl Tapp Jr. (September 9, 1977 – November 26, 2003), known by his stage name Soulja Slim, was an American rapper and songwriter known for the U.S. number-one hit “Slow Motion” by Juvenile. Like Jay Electronica, Soulja Slim comes from the infamous Magnolia Projects in New Orleans.

Tapp was murdered on November 26, 2003, after an assailant shot him four times, three in the face and once in the chest, on the front lawn of the home of his mother and stepfather.
