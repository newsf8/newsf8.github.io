Why Do Bollywood Movies Glorify Non-Consensual Touching During Holi

Holi is here, which means a lot of us will be streaming Bollywood songs as we apply gulaal and celebrate the festival of colours. However, before you make a playlist, it’s time to revisit some old favourites and understand how they are extremely problematic. After all, over the years, Bollywood has produced several movies that have glorified non-consensual touching and groping during Holi. 1. Silsila This iconic song is synonymous with Holi. In fact, if you have grown up listening to Bollywood songs, you’d know that this is the first song on most people’s minds during this time of the year. However, in the song, Amitabh Bachchan’s character is seen forcing Rekha’s character into playing Holi or dancing. In one shot, she’s seen standing in the middle of a group of men while they throw colours at her. If this is Bollywood’s idea of fun, they have some serious unlearning to do. 2. Kati Patang A portion of the lyrics say: “Han aaj na chodenge bas Humjoli khelenge hum holi Khelenge hum holi Chahe bhige re teri chunariya Chahe bhige re choli Khelenge hum holi” Even in the song, you can see an inebriated Rajesh Khanna touching a female dancer without her consent. He proceeds to shove her towards a group of men who treat her like a ragdoll. We can’t fathom what the makers were thinking during the making of this song. 3. Mohabbatein One might argue that the 70s and 80s weren’t as progressive as the 2000s. Well then, let’s take a look at this popular scene from the 2000 release Mohabbatein. In it, Uday Chopra’s character shows his machismo by manhandling Shamita Shetty’s character in the song and forcefully applying colours on her. Oh and the lyrics go: “Ja kudiye! Jo kar! Poora badan tera rang diya…” Yep! 4. Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani In this hit song, Naina (Deepika Padukone) first splashes water at Bunny (Ranbir Kapoor) who then proceeds to smear colours on her. The problem with this ‘fun’ approach is that it normalises non-consensual touching and groping. In fact, many women fear going out during Holi because they are afraid of getting molested or hurt. However, Bollywood seems to be blissfully unaware of the consequences of their Holi sequences. Here’s hoping that the new crop of directors and writers will do away with this normalisation. On that note, Happy Holi!
