Hajj Balloting Results 2020 Announced – Successful Candidates List

Religious Ministry has announced the Hajj Balloting Results 2020. Federal Minister for Religious Affairs Noor-ul-Haq Qadri announced the Hajj Balloting results in a press conference. The names are picked through transparent balloting conducted by Minister for Religious Affairs and Interfaith Harmony Sahibzada Noor-ul-Haq Qadri, said

Hajj Balloting Results 2020 Announced

The three successful candidates of Hajj Balloting are as follow:

Syed Shoaib Ahmed – Bahawalpur Muhammad Anwar Zulfi – Islamabad Kiramat Khan – Lakki Marwat

Federal Minister for Religious Affairs Noor-ul-Haq Qadri said that rest of the results will be sent to the applicants via SMS and will also be made available online at Minister for Religious Affairs official websites www.hajjinfo.org and www.mora.gov.pk.

BOL News will also update the Hajj Qurandazi 2020 results as soon as they are available.

The successful applicants have already been asked to submit their passports in their respective banks.

The unsuccessful applicants can take their money back from their respective banks as announced by the ministry

A group could be consisted of only one pilgrims and maximum of 14 aspirants..

The minister also said Pakistani Hajj pilgrims have been included in the road to Makkah project under which their customs, immigration would be held at Karachi, Lahore, Quetta and Islamabad airports

A total of 1,49,330 applications received under the Government Hajj Scheme. According to the Hajj Policy 2020, initially 179,210 Pakistanis to perform the sacred religious obligation. Around Some 10,000 seats under the government scheme reserved for senior citizens. Unsuccessful applicants in balloting continuously for the last three years will automatically be declared successful. 1000 seats are reserved for overseas Pakistanis.

The government Hajj package (per person) is Rs 463,445 for the north region and Rs 486,270 for the south region without Qurbani (sacrifice)

However Rs 486,270 and Rs 478,520 for north and south regions respectively with Qurbani.
