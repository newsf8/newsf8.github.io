Cafes and restaurants could shut with Goverment decision expected today -

Restaurants and cafes may be advised to close in the coming days as Simon Coveney addressed the issue this morning.

Coveney said that the Covid-19 special committee will be looking into the measure following the closure of pubs across the country over the weekend.

A decision is expected at the meeting at 2.30pm today.

Mr. Coveney told Neil Prendeville on Red FM that “as each day passes, decisions need to be made to protect people that are vulnerable.”

“Yesterday 368 people died in Italy. And we are going to reach a point in time where we are talking about fatalities on a daily basis in Ireland too.

“This is about saving lives. It’s about saving the lives of people we love. Old people, vulnerable people, people with disabilities, people with weak immune systems.

“These are the people that we all have to protect.”

Regarding restaurants and cafes potentially closing, Mr. Coveney said: “We are not going to leave that uncertainty hang for very long. We have our public health team meeting, and then we have our cabinet sub-committee meeting this afternoon at half past two.

“We will give clarity to all cafe and restaurant owners after that. Again, we will follow the public health advice. But people shouldn’t draw their own conclusions until decisions are made.”

The update from the Tanaiste follows the chief executive of the Restaurants Association of Ireland, Adrian Cummins, saying that restaurants should be forced to close to keep people safe.

“We believe that the government should issue an order for all restaurants and cafes to close in the best interests of public health,” Mr. Cummins said.

He added that up to 70,000 jobs would be affected by the move but believes it is in the best interests of the general public.

“We understand that the government has done as best they can and they are doing a very good job and they have actually said that they will make a decision in an orderly manner.

“We believe that this should be done sooner rather than later.”
