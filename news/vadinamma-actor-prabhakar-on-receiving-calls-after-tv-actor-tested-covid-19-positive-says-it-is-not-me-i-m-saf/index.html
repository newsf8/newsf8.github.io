Vadinamma actor Prabhakar on receiving calls after TV actor tested COVID-19 positive; says, “It is not me, I’m safe”

Telugu TV actor Boddu Prabhakar of Suryakantham and Mattigajulu fame reportedly tested positive for COVID-19. However, Vadinamma actor Prabhakar Podakandla, who also shares the same name, clarified that he is ‘safe and doing well’.He took to his Facebook handle to share a video in his stories adding that he has been receiving calls and messages from yesterday (June 23) enquiring about his well-being. Thanking everyone for the love and concern, Prabhakar mentioned that there are many named ‘Prabhakar’ in the industry but many have mistaken it to be him. In the video that was shot in the sets of Vadinamma, Prabhakar stated that the show’s shoot is currently under progress following all the guidelines and he is taking enough care as his life matters to him the most.Prabhakar is popular as a TV actor, anchor and producer. He recently went on to make his directorial debut as well. The multi-faceted actor came back to acting and his show Vadinamma which features Sujitha Dhanush, Priyanka Naidu, Mahi Shivan and others, has been doing well on the TRP charts before lockdown.Makers of Vadinamma have resumed shooting very recently and actress Priyanka Naidu has also shared a video on her Instagram handle offering a peek into their shoot post lockdown. Fresh episodes of the show are likely to go air from June 29 (Monday).

“Artistes Association of Telugu Television (AATT) directs actors to stop shooting amid Corona scare; set to discuss with Producers Council to find ‘long term’ solutions”

After a TV actor reportedly tested positive for COVID-19 , the Artistes Association of Telugu Television (AATT) has directed all the TV actors to stop shooting for their shows.AATT President Vinod Bala has communicated the same to the members of the association a few hours ago. Telugu Television Producers Council (TTPC) is scheduled to meet at 11 AM today (June 24) to discuss the current shooting scenario and a decision is likely to be announced soon.Speaking to ETimes TV about the same, President Vinod Bala, said, “All the actors are panic-stricken after learning about a TV actor testing positive for COVID-19. So we at AATT, have decided to stop attending TV shoots, discuss with the Producers Council the various aspects involved in shooting amid the crisis situation and take some key decisions that will benefit everyone in the long term.“He adds, “I’ve personally spoken to the actor who was tested positive. He is feeling low and is worried about the treatment as well as the expenses. I assured him that the Artistes Association will stand by him and that he need not worry. We are confident that he did not contract the disease on the shooting sets. He came from Tirupati and he might have developed symptoms during the journey. However, he participated in nearly 3 TV shootings and all the 120 members who were involved are currently worried. They must take COVID-19 tests at the earliest.“Elaborating on the issues to be discussed in the meeting with the Producers Council, Vinod Bala said, “Right from reviewing the sanitising procedures, involving actors from other states to deciding on the medical insurance for the actors, technicians and arriving at a consensus on the compensation to be given to the deceased, we are keen upon finding long term solutions for the issues involved.“Replying to the question on the delay in discussing the above, Bala said, “Right from crew members to many actors, many were badly affected by the lockdown and the financial situation commanded the decision to start shootings as soon as we were permitted to resume work. But now when it finally hit our family, everyone is naturally worried. In a way, this is a wake up to chalk out long term solutions in the interest of everyone.”

“Lakshmi Baramma: Lakshmi Baramma fame Chandan Kumar, Rashmi Prabhakar, and Chandu Gowda reunite in Hyderabad”

Kannada actors Chandan Kumar Rashmi Prabhakar , and Chandu Gowda , who mostly stay busy with a hectic schedule, have managed to fetch sometime for a quick reunion. The trio seemed to have caught up after a long time amidst their shooting schedule in Hyderabad.Chandu shared a picture on his Instagram handle, where they are seen posing for a selfie. The update shared by the actor has gone viral and has taken social media on the storm. Fans are happy to see their favourite stars together and many of them have been sharing the picture.On the other hand, although the actors have been acting in different shows at present, the fact the all of them featured in a popular Kananda daily soap ' Lakshmi Baramma ' is worth a mention.While Rashmi replaced actress Kavitha Gowda and played the titular role of ' Lacchi ' aka ‘Chinnu’, Chandan and Chandu played the male protagonists. Initially, when the show premiered, Chandhan Kumar played the lead character of ‘Chandu’.However, the actor bid adieu to the show in midway. Needless to say, by the end of the show, it was Chandu Gowda, who played the male protagonist.Currently, the trio has been acting in different Telugu shows. Rashmi features is a show titled ‘Pournami’, Chandhan plays the male protagonists in a daily show ‘Saavitrammagari abbai’ and Chandu is seen playing the lead role in ‘Trinayani’.
