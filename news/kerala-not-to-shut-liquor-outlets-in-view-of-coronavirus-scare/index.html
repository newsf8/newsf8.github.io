Kerala not to shut liquor outlets in view of coronavirus scare

With eight more cases of novel coronavirus reported in the state on Tuesday, Kerala has swung into action with the government deciding to impose severe restrictions, including closure of educational institutions and cinemas till the month end. There were reports that the Kerala State Beverages Corporation will also close down the retail outlets till March 31. However, in what came as a reprieve to tipplers in the state, the corporation clarified that no such decision has been taken as of now. In a press release, the corporation also warned against those spreading fake news on social media. With eight more people testing positive on Tuesday, the total number of infected people now under treatment touched 14 in the state. The parents of the three-year-old boy, who tested positive for COVID-19 in Kochi on Monday on the family's return from Italy, have also tested positive for the virus, Health minister K.K. Shailaja told reporters on Tuesday night. Kerala has been hit by the second round of coronavirus cases after the country's first three positive cases in the state were successfully cured and discharged from hospitals last month. Vijayan said schools and colleges and cinemas would remain closed in the state till March 31. Classes from I to VII standards of all schools -- state, CBSE and ICSE boards -- will remain shut and examinations which have begun for them will be put on hold. Examinations for class X, Plus Two and vocational higher secondary will however, continue. Final examinations of class eight and ninth also will proceed as usual. Madrasas, aganwadis, tution classes and tutorials, will be closed till March 31 while drama theatres and art and cultural festivals should be avoided. Temple and church festivals, which witnesses mass gatherings should be avoided, but rituals can be held in a low key manner.
