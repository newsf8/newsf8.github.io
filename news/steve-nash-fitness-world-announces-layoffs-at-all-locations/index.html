Steve Nash Fitness World announces layoffs at all locations

Earlier this month, Steve Nash Fitness World and Sports Clubs closed its locations due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

The announcement was made on March 17 and applied to 24 locations across British Columbia, including Fitness Worlds, Sports Clubs, and UFC Gyms. In an initial statement, Steve Nash said that team members would be paid during the first two weeks of the closure to “lessen the financial burden and impact.”

Now, the company says that they’ve been forced to lay off their staff.

“We have had to make the difficult move to let go of our valued team members as we transition through an organizational restructuring during these tough and unprecedented times,” reads the company’s social media post.

﻿

It’s unclear how many employees were terminated in this process.

Steve Nash says that all customer memberships have been frozen and that they will be accessible when the company reopens.

Daily Hive reached out to Steve Nash Fitness World but did not receive a response by time of publication.

COVID-19: Steve Nash Fitness World lets go of employees at its 24 locations across B.C.

As the coronavirus pandemic continues on, one of British Columbia’s largest fitness chains has of its employees.

Steve Nash Fitness World and Sports Club previously announced on March 17 that it would temporarily close its 24 locations until March 31 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

All memberships had been frozen and the company added that all staff would be paid during this time period. Online fitness instruction was being offered through their social media and website.

However, the company’s management team announced today (March 25) on its social media as well on its website that it has decided to “let go of our valued team members” as the company undergoes “organizational restructuring”.

The website message also states that updates will be provided as the company monitors the situation and continues to refer to their current status as a “temporary closure”.

According to several reports, staff received emails from human resources that they had been “terminated” as of March 24.

The company has 24 locations across B.C., including Victoria, Nanaimo, and Kelowna.

In the Lower Mainland, it has locations in Vancouver, North Vancouver, West Vancouver, Richmond, Burnaby, Coquitlam, Surrey, Delta, Langley, and Abbotsford.

Previously, Vancouver’s Semperviva Yoga announced on on March 18 that it was shutting down its four locations after 25 years in operation due to the financial strain caused by the pandemic.

One of British Columbia’s largest fitness providers has terminated all of its team members.

The decision on Tuesday follows a public announcement last week from Steve Nash Fitness World & Sports Clubs that the company had closed all its fitness facilities until March 31 to help B.C.‘s efforts to slow the spread of COVID-19.

On Tuesday, the company wrote in a letter to workers that its 26 Steve Nash-branded clubs, UFC clubs and Crunch Fitness locations – all operated by SNFW Fitness B.C. Ltd. – are closed indefinitely.

“The indefinite closure of our clubs… due to the COVID-19 pandemic has resulted in many difficult decisions for the company,” the letter states.

“While the company has explored all options to continue operations and protect our team members continued employment, we do not see a way forward at this time.”

In a public social media post about the chain’s closures last week, SNFW Fitness B.C. president and CEO Chris Smith said that all team members would be paid during the first two weeks of the shutdown to lessen workers’ financial burden.

In a letter dated March 24, the company noted that more information regarding final pay has yet to come.
