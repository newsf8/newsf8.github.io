Perrie Edwards shares insight into isolation with Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain

They have been isolating together in their plush pad.

And Perrie Edwards has shared an insight to what it’s like being housebound with her boyfriend Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain during Friday’s episode of Good Morning Britain.

The Little Mix star, 26, playfully said that living with her beau at the moment is like being with a child after they have been given ‘Coca-Cola and sweets’.

Isolation: Perrie Edwards has shared an insight to what it’s like being housebound with her boyfriend Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain during Friday’s episode of Good Morning Britain

Perrie appeared on video link from their home as she spoke to hosts Ben Shepherd and Lorraine Kelly amid the ongoing coronavirus outbreak.

Her beau Alex typically spends his day training at Liverpool Football Club, however premiere league sides are currently suspended and not expected to play until June.

Perrie joked that Alex has lots of extra energy and said: ‘It’s like giving a child have Coca Cola and a whole bag of sweets and putting them in a room and keeping them in there. He’s got so much energy!’

However generally she admitted things have been running smoothly, adding: ‘Actually really well, not too bad. We’re just trying to stay positive and keep ourselves busy and I’ve just been doing loads of stuff like knitting, a bit of baking, random stuff I never normally have time for.’

Perrie and Alex kept their followers entertained earlier this week by sharing a Tik Tok video of the two of them dancing on their kitchen stairs.

Discussing how she learned the steps to the viral video, Perrie said: ‘It took me a good while. Alex learned straight away because he’s annoyingly good at everything, so he got it straight away and I just kept getting it wrong.

‘He’s really good at stuff like that, but I kept getting scared on the stairs, like if I fell and broke an ankle. But it was really fun when we got it, it was brilliant.’

The star also revealed that the group are going to go ahead and release their new single Break Up Song in a bid to ‘life everyone’s spirits’ in the current climate.

She said: ‘It’s such a weird time for everybody. Luckily for us we had the single sorted, we had everything ready to go. We were going to hold off releasing it, but it’s a nice positive, uplifting song, it’ll make everyone feel good.’

Although she’s been spending more time with her boyfriend, Perrie has been isolated from her band mates for the past couple of weeks.

However she assured fans they are constantly in touch, and said: ‘Our group chat on WhatsApp is always popping off. We’re so used to being together 24/7, we live in each other’s pockets.

‘When we’re not together, it feels really weird. But it’s also quite nice to have a little bit of breathing space for once because we never get time off. It’s just weird circumstances.’

Earlier during a radio appearance on Heart Breakfast with Amanda Holden and Jamie Theakston, Perrie revealed while the single will be released their album is on hold.

She said: ‘I feel like it’s [the single] such a good upbeat positive uplifting song and that’s just what people need right now, but with the album, I think we are going to have to postpone it yeah.’

Amanda suggested that the band could release it later in the summer or in the autumn, before Perrie agreed and said ‘yeah.’

Perrie Edwards formed an unlikely lockdown friendship with Katharine McPhee and David Foster as the trio video chatted for Katharine’s birthday.

Turning 36 in isolation on Wednesday, the stage and TV actress roped in her 70-year-old husband for a birthday celebration which she shared with fans on Instagram Live.

And one of the unlikely famous faces to join the couple was Perrie, 26, who serenaded Katharine with a version of The Simpsons’ infamous Happy Birthday Lisa song (with Katharine’s name in it, of course…)

Explaining that they’d never actually met IRL but were just fans of each other from Instagram, the birthday girl had a fangirl moment as she told Perrie: ‘We listened to Little Mix today in the car. We went out for five minutes today and we listened to Little Mix.’

Perrie Edwards has opened up about what she has been up to while self-isolating.

The Little Mix singer and her boyfriend Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain have opted to isolate in London amid the coronavirus outbreak.

The Liverpool Echo reports the pair decided to stay in the capital after the Premier League season was suspended until at least April 30.

Speaking to Kiss radio, Perrie said: “I have a house – well, my boyfriend and I have a house in Manchester and a house in London, so we were originally in Manchester together and then we decided to come to London.

“I kind of wish we’d stayed in Manchester rather than London ‘cos there’s more to do there, like in his house.

“But we have got a lovely house in London with a little garden so Hatchi’s alright. It’s just weird, isn’t it? Not being able to leave the house, because I have been isolating for ages now.”

Asked what she has been up to while of precautionary self-isolation, Perrie told: “I haven’t been doing any exercise! I’ve been watching loads of Netflix, I started Money Heist which is unreal.

“I’ve been singing a little bit, I’ve been cooking a lot because my boyfriend is never full, like try isolating with an athlete!

“Trying to ration your food and every five seconds ‘I’m hungry! I’m hungry again. Can I have some food now?’ I’m like, seriously?

“I’ve been baking, which I never do. I baked a banana bread cake the other day.

“It’s the best cake nobody will ever be able to taste. We were FaceTiming all our family like ‘if only you could taste this right now’.

A video shared to popular app TikTok earlier this month, which showed the couple dancing in unison, quickly went viral.

In response to the clip, Perrie added: “And TikToking – I absolutely love TikTok, I can’t believe I haven’t had it until now.”

The popstar was also asked whether the pair had had any rows during isolation. The 26-year-old said: “I’m probably this close to burning, or just cutting straight through the wire of the PlayStation.

“There’s going to be a situation here.

“Honestly, even just sat in the lounge trying to Netflix and chill there’s just ‘It’s behind you mate behind you urghhh’.

“I’m like ‘Stop with the PlayStation for two seconds!'”

Little Mix are set to release their new single entitled Break Up Song this Friday.
