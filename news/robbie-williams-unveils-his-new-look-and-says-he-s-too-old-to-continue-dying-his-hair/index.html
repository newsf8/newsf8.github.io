Robbie Williams unveils his new look and says he's 'too old' to continue dying his hair

Robbie Williams has unveiled an unexpected new look, arguing that he’s too old to keep colouring his hair.

The ‘Angels’ singer debuted his grey buzzcut in Australia on Thursday (March 12) ahead of performing at the Lakeside Stadium in Melbourne as part of his World Tour at the weekend.

Sam Tabone Getty Images

Earlier this week, the former Take That singer and X Factor judge explained that he shaved his barnet off in an attempt to avoid looking like “the rock stars of yesterday”.

“There’s a certain age that you reach where you go, ‘I better stop dying this stuff’,” the 46-year-old continued (via The Sun). “You don’t want to have that auburn red tip at the end.”

Interestingly enough, trying his best not to look like an ageing musician wasn’t the only preventative measure Robbie was taking while spending some time Down Under.

Rather than offering hugs and handshakes to those who approached him, the singer-songwriter favoured elbow bumps amid global coronavirus fears.

Despite his best efforts though, the World Health Organisation’s Director-General Dr Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus has just recently stated that even that method of saying hello to people could spread germs, as they still put you within one metre of the other person.
